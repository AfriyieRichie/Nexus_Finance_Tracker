Introduction & System Overview

1.1 About Nexus Finance Tracker

Nexus Finance Tracker is an enterprise-grade, cloud-hosted financial management system designed to serve the complete accounting and financial reporting needs of modern organisations. Built on International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) and International Accounting Standards (IAS), the system provides a unified platform for managing every aspect of an organisation’s financial life — from day-to-day bookkeeping through to statutory reporting.

The system is built as a multi-tenant, multi-currency, multi-organisation platform, meaning a single deployment can serve multiple independent organisations simultaneously, each with complete data isolation, their own chart of accounts, their own user base, and their own reporting calendar.

1.2 Key Design Principles

PrincipleDescription
Double-Entry AccountingEvery financial transaction is recorded with balanced debits and credits. The system enforces this at all entry points.
Period ControlFinancial periods are explicitly managed. Once a period is closed and locked, no posting can alter it, preserving the integrity of historical statements.
Segregation of DutiesThe RBAC model ensures that the person who creates a transaction cannot also be the one who approves and posts it.
AuditabilityEvery action in the system — from a password change to a journal reversal — is recorded in an immutable audit trail.
IFRS ComplianceFinancial statements are presented in accordance with IAS 1, IAS 2, IAS 16, IAS 36, and related standards.
Ghana Statutory CompliancePayroll module is pre-configured with Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) PAYE tax bands, SSNIT contribution rates, and bonus tax treatment.

User Roles & Permissions

3.1 Role Hierarchy

Nexus Finance Tracker implements a nine-tier role-based access control (RBAC) system. Roles are assigned per user per organisation.

SUPER_ADMIN
    └── ORG_ADMIN
            └── FINANCE_MANAGER
                    ├── APPROVER
                    ├── ACCOUNTANT
                    │       ├── ACCOUNTS_PAYABLE_CLERK
                    │       └── ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE_CLERK
                    ├── AUDITOR
                    └── REPORT_VIEWER

3.2 Role Descriptions

ORG_ADMIN — Organisation Administrator

Full access to all functions within the organisation.

  • Create and manage user accounts and roles
  • Lock/unlock accounting periods; perform year-end close
  • Delete accounts from the chart of accounts
  • Unlock locked bank reconciliations; approve budgets

FINANCE_MANAGER

Responsible for overall financial integrity. Can post and reverse journal entries.

  • Post and reverse journal entries
  • Manage the chart of accounts (create/edit, not delete)
  • Approve inventory movements; run and post depreciation
  • Post stocktake variances; close accounting periods

APPROVER

Reviews and authorises transactions submitted via approval workflows.

  • Approve or reject journal entries, invoices, payroll runs
  • Delegate approval authority; view notifications

ACCOUNTANT

Handles day-to-day transaction entry. Creates transactions but cannot post without approval.

  • Create and edit journal entries (DRAFT status)
  • Submit transactions for approval
  • Create inventory movements; record AR/AP payments

ACCOUNTS_PAYABLE_CLERK

Specialist role focused on supplier invoice processing and payment.

ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE_CLERK

Specialist role focused on customer invoicing, collections, and customer statements.

AUDITOR

Read-only access to the audit trail and all financial records. Cannot create or modify records.

REPORT_VIEWER

Can view all financial reports and records but cannot create or modify anything.

Accounting Periods

6.1 Period Status Workflow

OPEN → CLOSED → LOCKED
StatusDescription
OPENTransactions can be posted to this period.
CLOSEDNo new transactions. Can be reopened by ORG_ADMIN.
LOCKEDPermanently sealed. Cannot be reopened. Audit-compliant.

6.2 Creating a Fiscal Year

A fiscal year creates all 12 monthly periods at once.

  1. Navigate to Settings → Periods.
  2. Click Create Fiscal Year.
  3. Enter the fiscal year number (e.g., 2026) and the start date.
  4. Click Create. The system automatically creates 12 periods.

6.3 Closing and Locking Periods

Closing (reversible): Navigate to Settings → Periods, find the period, click Close Period. Prevents new postings; can be reopened.

Locking (irreversible): Only after all journals are posted, bank reconciliation is complete, and statements are signed off. Click Lock Period on a closed period. Only ORG_ADMIN can do this.

⛔
Warning: A locked period cannot be unlocked. Any correction must be made in the current open period as an adjusting entry.

6.4 Year-End Close

Year-end close finalises the fiscal year, locks all periods, posts a CLOSING journal to transfer net income/loss to Retained Earnings, and resets revenue and expense accounts to zero for the new year. Navigate to Settings → Periods → Year-End Close. Requires ORG_ADMIN permission and cannot be reversed.

7.3 Creating a Journal Entry

  1. Navigate to Journals → New Journal Entry.
  2. Complete the Header: Type, Date, Accounting Period, Reference, Description, Currency, Exchange Rate (if foreign currency).
  3. Add a minimum of 2 Journal Lines. For each line, select the Account and enter either a Debit or Credit (not both).
  4. The totals at the bottom must be balanced (Total Debits = Total Credits).
  5. Click Save as Draft or Submit for Approval.
ℹ
Rule: Every journal entry must be balanced. The system will not allow you to save or submit an unbalanced entry.

7.4 Multi-Currency Entries

Select the foreign Currency, enter the Exchange Rate (units of base currency per 1 unit of foreign currency), and enter line amounts in the foreign currency. The system automatically calculates the base currency equivalent.

7.5 Reversing a Posted Journal

  1. Open the POSTED journal entry.
  2. Click Reverse.
  3. Enter the Reversal Date, Period, and optional description.
  4. Click Create Reversal. The system creates a REVERSAL entry with debits and credits swapped.

7.6 Journal Numbering

Journals are automatically numbered using the format JE-YYYY-NNNNN — e.g., JE-2026-00047 is the 47th journal entry dated in 2026.

Bank Reconciliation

10.1 Reconciliation Process

  1. Set up bank accounts: Bank → Accounts → Add Bank Account. Link each bank account to its GL chart account.
  2. Import a statement: Click on a bank account → Import Statement → upload CSV file → confirm opening/closing balances.
  3. Match transactions: The matching screen shows two panels — statement lines (left) and unmatched GL entries (right). Use Auto-Match for system-suggested matches or manually select and click Match.
  4. Create journals for unmatched lines: For bank charges or other statement lines with no GL entry, click Create Journal and post.
  5. Confirm reconciliation: Once all lines are matched, click Confirm Reconciliation. The reconciliation is locked.
ℹ
Once a reconciliation is confirmed, it is locked. Only ORG_ADMIN can unlock it for corrections.

10.2 CSV Statement Format

Your bank statement CSV must include columns for: Date (YYYY-MM-DD or DD/MM/YYYY), Description/Narration, Debit Amount, Credit Amount, and Reference (optional).

Fixed Assets

📋
Compliant with IAS 16 (Property, Plant and Equipment) and IAS 36 (Impairment of Assets). Manages the full asset lifecycle from acquisition through disposal.

11.2 Asset Categories

Create categories to group assets with similar accounting treatment. Navigate to Assets → Categories → New Category.

Creating an Asset Category

Each category requires the following fields:

  • Asset Account — The balance sheet account recording the asset at cost.
  • Accumulated Depreciation Account — Contra asset account accumulating periodic depreciation.
  • Depreciation Expense Account — P&L account for periodic depreciation charges.
  • Gain/Loss on Disposal Account — P&L account for gains and losses on asset disposal.
  • Retained Earnings Account — Equity account used when automatically transferring revaluation surplus to retained earnings each depreciation period (IAS 16.41). Optional.

11.3 Asset Records

Navigate to Assets → Asset Register → New Asset. Complete the asset record with the following information.

Financial Details

  • Cost — The original acquisition cost of the asset.
  • Residual Value — Estimated scrap or salvage value at end of useful life.
  • Useful Life (Months) — Total expected useful life in months (used for Straight-Line and other time-based methods).
  • Depreciation Method — Straight-Line, Reducing Balance, Sum of Years Digits, or Units of Production.
  • Reducing Balance Rate (optional, Reducing Balance method only) — Annual rate as a decimal (e.g., 0.25 for 25%). If omitted, Double Declining Balance is used.
  • Acquisition Date — The date the asset was placed into service.
  • Asset Category — Links the asset to the correct GL accounts.

11.4 Depreciation Methods

MethodFormulaBest For
Straight-Line(Cost − Residual) ÷ Useful Life (years)Buildings, furniture
Reducing BalanceCarrying Amount × Rate% (user-defined or Double Declining Balance)Motor vehicles, IT equipment
Sum of Years Digits(Cost − Residual) × (Remaining Life ÷ Sum of Digits)Assets with front-loaded benefits
Units of ProductionUnits Used × [(Cost − Residual) ÷ Total Units]Plant, mining equipment

11.5 Running Depreciation

  1. Navigate to Assets → Depreciation → Run Depreciation.
  2. Select the Accounting Period.
  3. Click Preview to review calculated charges without posting.
  4. Click Post Depreciation to create and post the DEPRECIATION journal entry.
Automatic Journal — Depreciation Run
Depreciation Expense AccountDR
Accumulated Depreciation AccountCR

First-Period Proration

For assets acquired mid-month, the first depreciation charge is automatically prorated by the number of days the asset was active in that month (IAS 16 compliant). Subsequent periods receive a full monthly charge.

Depreciation Schedule

To project future depreciation without posting: Open the asset record → click Depreciation Schedule → enter the number of months to project (default 60). The system returns a period-by-period table showing projected depreciation charge, accumulated depreciation, and carrying value. This is read-only and does NOT post any journals.

11.6 Reversing Depreciation

Navigate to Assets → Depreciation → Runs → find the run → click Reverse. The system posts a reversing journal. Once reversed, you can re-run depreciation with corrected parameters.

11.8 Impairment (IAS 36)

When the recoverable amount of an asset falls below its carrying amount: Open asset → Impair Asset → enter the following fields → Post Impairment.

  • Recoverable Amount — The higher of fair value less costs to sell and value in use. The system computes the impairment loss as Carrying Value − Recoverable Amount.
  • Impairment Date / Period — Date and accounting period to post the impairment charge.
  • Impairment Loss Account — The P&L account to debit for the impairment charge.

11.9 Impairment Reversal (IAS 36.111)

When circumstances that caused an impairment loss have reversed:

  1. Open the asset record → click Reverse Impairment.
  2. Enter:
    • Reversal Date and Period
    • Reversal Amount — Cannot exceed the original impairment loss, and the restored carrying value cannot exceed what it would have been had no impairment occurred.
    • Impairment Account — P&L account to credit the recovery.
  3. Click Post Reversal.
Automatic Journal — Impairment Reversal
Asset / Accumulated Depreciation Account (restoring carrying value)DR
Impairment Reversal (P&L)CR

The reversal is recorded in the impairment reversals register.

11.10 Revaluation Surplus Transfer (IAS 16.41)

When a revalued asset is subsequently depreciated, a portion of the revaluation surplus is automatically transferred to Retained Earnings each period during the depreciation run. No manual action is required.

  • Transfer amount = Revaluation Surplus Remaining ÷ Remaining Useful Life Periods.
  • The Retained Earnings Account configured on the asset category is used for this posting.
Automatic Journal — Revaluation Surplus Transfer (per depreciation period)
Revaluation Reserve Account (equity)DR
Retained Earnings Account (equity)CR

11.11 Bulk Asset Import

To import multiple assets at once, navigate to Assets → Import. Download the CSV template, populate it with asset data, and upload. The system validates each row and reports any errors before creating asset records. Ensure each asset’s Category is pre-configured before importing.

Inventory Management

📋
Compliant with IAS 2 (Inventories). Supports FIFO, Weighted Average, and Standard Cost methods with multi-location tracking and full GL integration.

12.1 Costing Methods

MethodDescriptionBest For
Weighted Average (AVCO)Unit cost is the running weighted average of all purchasesGeneral merchandise, commodities
FIFOFirst In, First Out — oldest costs expensed firstPerishables, items with expiry dates
Standard CostA pre-set cost used regardless of purchase priceManufacturing, stable-cost items
⚠
Warning: The costing method cannot be changed once movements have been recorded on an item.

12.2 Creating an Inventory Item

  1. Navigate to Inventory → Stock Items → New Item.
  2. Enter SKU/Code, Name, Category, Unit of Measure, Opening Unit Cost, Reorder Level/Quantity, and Costing Method.
  3. Set GL Account Links: Inventory Control Account (asset) and COGS/Cost of Sales Account (expense).
  4. Click Save Item.
★
Important: GL Account Links must be configured before any inventory movement can be posted to the general ledger. Without these, stock balances update but no accounting entry is created.

12.3 GL Account Links

Configure the following GL accounts on each inventory item to enable automatic journal posting:

  • Inventory Control Account — Balance sheet asset account holding the stock value.
  • COGS / Cost of Sales Account — P&L expense account charged when stock is issued.
  • Purchase Price Variance (PPV) Account (Standard Cost items only) — Account to absorb the difference between standard and actual purchase price.
💡
Export CSV: The Items tab has an Export CSV button that downloads the currently filtered item list (respecting any active search filter) with columns: SKU, Name, Description, Category, Costing Method, Unit, Unit Cost, Qty on Hand, Stock Value, Reorder Level, Status.

12.6 Retroactive GL Posting (Post GL)

If a movement was created without a Contra Account or Period (no GL posted at the time), you can retroactively post it:

  1. Pre-requisite: The item must have an Inventory Control Account set. Edit the item via Stock Items → Edit if not already configured.
  2. Navigate to Inventory → Movements tab.
  3. Find the POSTED movement with an orange Post GL button.
  4. Click Post GL, select Contra Account and Accounting Period.
  5. Click Post Journal.

12.7 Physical Stocktake

  1. Create session: Inventory → Stocktake → New Stocktake. Enter session name, count date, location, and notes.
  2. Enter counts: Open the session → Enter Counts. For each item, enter the Counted Quantity.
  3. Review variances: The session shows System Quantity, Counted Quantity, Variance, and Variance Value.
  4. Post variances: Select the Accounting Period AND the Contra Account (required for GL posting of variance movements) → Post Variances. The system creates STOCKTAKE_IN/OUT movements and posts to GL.
ℹ
Cancelling a Stocktake: Cancel is available for OPEN or COUNTING sessions. A session that has already been posted cannot be cancelled.

12.8 Inventory Valuation Report

Navigate to Inventory → Valuation tab. The report shows Quantity on Hand, Unit Cost, and Total Value per item. The Grand Total should agree with the Inventories line on the Balance Sheet.

12.9 NRV Write-Down (IAS 2.9)

Under IAS 2, inventories must be measured at the lower of cost or Net Realisable Value (NRV). When NRV falls below current cost:

  1. Navigate to Inventory → Stock Items tab.
  2. Select the item → click NRV Write-Down.
  3. Enter:
    • NRV per Unit — The estimated net realisable value per unit.
    • Write-Down Expense Account — P&L account to debit.
    • Accounting Period — Period to post the journal to.
    • Location (optional) — Limit write-down to a specific storage location.
    • Notes — Reason for write-down for audit purposes.
  4. Click Post Write-Down.
Automatic Journal — NRV Write-Down
Write-Down Expense Account (P&L)DR
Inventory AccountCR

The item’s unit cost and total stock value are updated to NRV in all affected stock balances.

  3. Click Submit for Approval. The ORG_ADMIN reviews and clicks Approve.

13.4 Budget vs Actual — Variance Report

Open the budget → Variance Report. The report shows Budget, Actual, Variance, Variance%, YTD Budget, YTD Actual, and YTD Variance per account per period. Items over budget by a configured threshold are highlighted.

  • Fiscal-Year Scope: Actuals are scoped to the budget’s fiscal year only. GL entries posted outside the budget’s fiscal year are excluded from the report.
  • Period Breakdown (byPeriod=true): When this query option is enabled, the report returns one row per account × cost centre × period (instead of totals only), enabling per-month analysis and drill-down into individual periods.

13.5 Commitments

Commitments represent legally committed but not yet invoiced expenditure (purchase orders, contracts). They reduce the available budget balance. Navigate to the budget → Commitments tab → New Commitment.

15.2 Tax Codes

Deleting a Tax Code

⚠
A tax code that is referenced by any existing journal line cannot be deleted. Deactivate it instead using the isActive toggle. Deactivated codes remain on historical transactions but cannot be used on new ones.

15.3 VAT Returns

Generating a VAT Return

  1. Navigate to Tax → VAT Returns → Generate Return.
  2. Select the accounting period. The system aggregates all posted transactions with VAT codes.
  3. Review Output Tax (collected from customers), Input Tax (paid to suppliers), and Net VAT Payable/Refundable.
  4. Update status to SUBMITTED and then FILED when submitted to GRA.
  • Transactions with WITHHOLDING treatment are excluded from VAT returns. Withholding tax is income tax, not VAT, and is reported separately.
  • The system prevents generating a new VAT return if a SUBMITTED or FILED return already covers an overlapping date range.

15.4 FX Revaluation

At period end, revalue foreign currency balances to the closing exchange rate to recognise unrealised FX gains/losses (IAS 21.28).

  1. Navigate to Tax → FX Revaluation → New Revaluation.
  2. Select the Period and FX Gain/Loss Account (P&L account for the exchange difference).
  3. Enter the Period (the accounting period to post the journal to), the FX Gain/Loss Account (P&L account for the exchange difference), and the Closing Exchange Rate.
  4. Click Post Revaluation.
ℹ
The system automatically creates and posts an ADJUSTMENT journal entry (IAS 21.28) for all foreign currency account revaluation differences. The journal is linked to the revaluation record for full traceability.

Reversing an FX Revaluation

The system posts a full reversing journal entry and marks the original as REVERSED with the reversal journal reference stored against the original revaluation record.

Appendix B — Tips & Best Practices

Journal Entry Tips

  • The totals at the bottom of the journal entry turn green when balanced — this is your confirmation before submitting.
  • Use the Tab key to move between fields when entering many journal lines.
  • The header Description propagates to all lines if no individual line description is entered. Add line-level descriptions for complex entries.
  • For recurring month-end entries (accruals), create the journal once, save as Draft, then copy it next month.

Inventory Tips

  • Ensure GL Account Links (Inventory Control Account and COGS Account) are set on every item before creating movements. Items without these links produce no GL entries.
  • Run the Valuation Report at month-end and compare the Grand Total to the Inventories line on your Balance Sheet. Any difference indicates unlinked or unposted movements.

Payroll Tips

  • Always run Preview before posting a payroll run. Verify totals against your manual estimate before committing.
  • Download the payment file before locking the run, as the file cannot be regenerated after locking.

Reports Tips

  • The Drilldown feature on Balance Sheet and Income Statement lets you click any line to see underlying account balances and then individual transactions.
  • Change the As-of Date on the Balance Sheet to any past date for a historical snapshot.
  • Use the Trial Balance before generating financial statements to catch unbalanced entries early.

Appendix C — Error Messages & Troubleshooting

“Journal entry is not balanced”

Cause: Total debits ≠ total credits.
Fix: Review each line. Ensure every line has either a debit or a credit, not both. Adjust until totals are equal.

“Period is closed — cannot post entries”

Cause: Posting to a closed or locked period.
Fix: Change the journal date to an open period, or ask ORG_ADMIN to reopen the period (if not locked).

“Only POSTED movements can have their GL retroactively posted”

Cause: Attempted Post GL on a movement that has not been fully processed.
Fix: Check the movement status. Only POSTED movements (green badge) with no journal entry (orange Post GL button) qualify.

“This item has no Inventory GL account configured”

Cause: The inventory item has no Inventory Control Account linked.
Fix: Go to Inventory → Stock Items → Edit the item → GL Account Links → set Inventory Control Account → Save Changes. Then retry Post GL.

“A locked reconciliation already exists for this date”

Cause: Attempting to reimport a statement for a locked reconciliation period.
Fix: Ask ORG_ADMIN to unlock the reconciliation (Bank → Statements → select statement → Unlock), then reimport.

“Cannot change cost method after movements have been recorded”

Cause: The costing method is immutable once movements exist.
Fix: Write off all existing stock (ADJUSTMENT_OUT), create a new item with the correct costing method, then raise a RECEIPT for the opening balance on the new item.

“Depreciation has already been run for period”

Cause: A depreciation run is already posted for the selected period.
Fix: Reverse the existing run first (Assets → Depreciation → Runs → Reverse), then re-run with correct parameters.

“An unexpected error occurred. Please try again later.”

Cause: A server-side error.
Fix: (1) Refresh and try again. (2) Verify all required fields are complete (especially GL accounts and accounting periods). (3) If the error persists, contact your system administrator with the time of the error, the module, and the action you were performing.

“Insufficient stock for item ‘X’”

Cause: Issuing more stock than is currently on hand.
Fix: Check current quantity on hand in Stock Items. Reduce the issue quantity, or receive additional stock first.

Getting Help

If you encounter an issue not covered here:

  1. Check the Audit Trail — navigate to Audit to see if an error event was logged with more detail.
  2. Check the Period — confirm the accounting period for the transaction is OPEN.
  3. Check Roles — confirm you have the correct role to perform the action (see Section 3.2).
  4. Contact Support — provide: your name and email, organisation name, module and action, exact error message, date and time, and steps to reproduce.
